**A Seeker of the Sun tribal language inspired by FFXIV Miqo'te naming conventions**

### Vowels

- **Front**: i iː
- **Central**: a aː
- **Back**: u uː, o oː, e eː

### Diphthongs

- **ae** - pronounced /æ/ as in "cat" (used in sacred names)
- **ai** - as in "aisle" (common in tribal speech)
- **ao** - as in "cow" (used in formal register)
- **ou** - as in "go" (used in poetic speech)
- **eu** - as in "feud" (rare, used only in spellcasting)

### Orthographic Rules

- **x** = /x/ (voiceless velar fricative, like "ch" in Scottish "loch")
- **'** = /ʔ/ (glottal stop)
- **ae** = /æ/ (ash vowel, as in "cat")
- **quo** = /oː/ (long o)
- **ai** = /ai/ (diphthong as in "aisle")
- **ao** = /ao/ (diphthong as in "cow")
- **ou** = /ou/ (diphthong as in "go")
- **eu** = /eu/ (diphthong as in "feud")
- No letter **c**; /t͡s/ written as **ts**
- Long vowels may be unmarked in casual writing
- Diphthongs are written as two vowels

### Syllable Structure

(C)(w)V(ʔ)(C) - preferring open syllables with CV patterns

## Grammar System: Four Speech Aspects

W'rhani uses four distinct grammatical aspects that change verb inflection, word order, and level of explicitness based on audience and context.

### 1. Divine/Spellcasting Aspect (Rha'sol)

**Used for**: Speaking to gods, casting spells, ritual invocation
**Characteristics**:

- No verb tense or declension
- Free word order, fragments allowed
- Verbs take only spell-type suffixes: **-xha** (healing), **-mha** (protection), **-'a** (purification), **-'o** (intensification)
- Subjects/objects omitted
- Highly metaphorical and poetic

**Example**: `Lio-'a... rha'lo... mwo...` ("Light purifies... heart-feeling... presence...")

### 2. Tribal/Intimate Aspect (Rhan'ti)

**Used for**: Speaking with tribe members, close family
**Characteristics**:

- Compressed, informal speech
- Verbs use **-we** (ongoing) or **-wha** (completed)
- Flexible S-V/V-S word order
- Subjects often implied by context
- Fast delivery with contractions

**Example**: `Rha'lo-we` ("I love you" - literally "feel-heart-ongoing")

### 3. Outsider-Informal Aspect (Se'aen)

**Used for**: Casual conversation with strangers, friendly outsiders
**Characteristics**:

- Clear but relaxed S-V-O word order
- Verbs use **-na** (general) or **-nari** (polite emphasis)
- Subjects/objects required for clarity
- Avoids heavy metaphor

**Example**: `W'zamqo rhen-na you` ("W'zamqo heals you")

### 4. Outsider-Formal Aspect (Sol'han)

**Used for**: Diplomatic, legal, ceremonial situations
**Characteristics**:

- Strict S-O-V word order only
- Verbs use **-tar** (formal) or **-tari** (ceremonial)
- Nouns marked: **-ra** (subject), **-ti** (object)
- Extremely precise, no ambiguity
- More rigid than legal proceedings

**Example**: `W'zamqo-ra you-ti rhen-tar` ("W'zamqo heals you" - formal)

## Key Morphological Features

### Noun Marking

- **-ra**: Subject marker (formal register only)
- **-ti**: Object marker (formal register only)
- No grammatical gender; biological sex optional

### Verb System

One verb root changes meaning through register-specific suffixes:

- **rhen**: heal → rhen-xha (heal!), rhen-we (is healing), rhen-na (heals), rhen-tar (formally heals)
- **rha**: feel deeply/love → rha'lo-we (I love you), Rha'lo xha'qo (ritual love oath)

### Pronouns

- **no**: I/me (dropped in R1-R2)
- **sa**: you (singular)
- **sarhi**: you (plural/group)
- **h'no**: we/us (tribe only, "all of us of the breath")

## Dictionary Core Lexicon

### Core Nouns

- **rhan**: breath, dawn, renewal
- **qo**: embrace, enfold
- **lo**: heart, core of being
- **mwo**: presence, recognition, greeting (root of "hello")
- **zam**: healer-mother/white-mage tradition
- **rhid**: wind-born, wanderer
- **lio**: light, brilliance
- **sola**: barrier, shield
- **xhun**: blight, corruption (taboo in ritual)

### Core Verbs

- **rhen**: to heal
- **xhu**: to cleanse/purify
- **xa**: to reject/push away
- **a'nhe**: to restore breath/life
- **mwo**: to greet/meet/recognize presence
- **rha**: to feel deeply, soul-feel (love)
- **sola**: to protect/shield
- **lio**: to illuminate/reveal truth

### Greetings & Social Phrases

- **I'mwo**: hello (neutral)
- **Mwo'rha**: hey, soul-friend (tribal only)
- **I'mwo-tari**: ceremonial greeting
- **Ko'a**: go well/farewell
- **Ko'a-whe**: go safely/protective farewell

### Love & Intimacy

- **rha'lo-we**: I love you (tribal, everyday)
- **Lo'rha... mwo'qo... rhan-xha...**: My heart embraces yours (poetic, spell aspect)
- **M'za rha'lo-we**: I love you, sun-heart (affectionate)
- **Rha'lo xha'qo W'rhan**: I love you completely (ritual binding)

### Swearing & Emotional Language

- **xha'to**: ash-rot ("damn it!")
- **mhex'ra**: sun-scourge (strong insult)
- **za'xhun**: blighted sand (severe taboo)
- **raxo**: foolish cub (playful insult)
- **mhen'cat**: mischief-cub (teasing, affectionate)

## Name Etymology: W'zamqo Rhid

### W'zamqo

- **W'**: dawn-born lineage marker
- **zam**: healer-mother/white-mage tradition
- **qo**: embrace, nurturing

**Meaning**: "The Dawn-Healer who Embraces" or "She who embraces with healing"

### Rhid

- Wind-born wanderer, indicating connection to movement and freedom

**Full meaning**: "The Dawn-Healer who Embraces, Wind-Born Wanderer"

## Cultural & Magical Principles

### Core Metaphors

- **Love = healing breath + heart**
- **Emotion = breath and light**
- **Identity = dawn lineage**
- **Magic = poetry, not mechanics**
- **Grammar tightens with social distance**

### Spellcasting Logic

Spell aspect avoids direct grammar. Magic works through:

- Elemental invocation first ("Light, breath, wind...")
- Noun chaining (lo'rhan'mwo = "heart of dawn's presence")
- Metaphor over literal meaning
- Lineage names as invocation (W'zamqo triggers healing emphasis)

### W'zamqo's Religious Perspective

- **Favored**: Azeyma (sun, boundaries, individual will)
- **Hated**: Nophica (represents self-sacrifice to nature, which W'zamqo rejects)
- **Pragmatic respect**: Nald'thal (recognizes costs of healing)
- **Rare use**: Menphina (prefers solar clarity over lunar tenderness)

This language reflects a culture where healing is both practical magic and spiritual practice, where personal boundaries matter as much as compassion, and where the sun's clear light is valued over nature's consuming growth.
